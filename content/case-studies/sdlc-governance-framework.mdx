## The Problem
Customers don't think in terms of security policies vs. compliance settings vs. project governance. They think: "I need to enforce rules across my SDLC." They want one place to define, enforce, and audit all of it, but the controls are scattered across 6+ different settings surfaces in GitLab.

## The Opportunity
GitLab Ultimate, which includes Security Policies, represents 56% of ARR and is a key driver in major enterprise deals. Positioning GitLab as the central "governance enforcement layer" INSIDE the developer workflow provides a powerful competitive advantage over tools like ServiceNow that operate ABOVE the SDLC. We had a chance to build a true platform capability that would be a significant competitive differentiator and a major driver of Ultimate adoption.

## My Process & What I Did
My goal was to take this from a high-level customer pain point to a concrete, actionable vision that the company could rally behind.

### 1. Gap Analysis & Vision Definition
I started by identifying the full scope of the problem. I conducted a gap analysis and identified **107 discrete gaps** across 7 categories that separated our current state from a true, unified SDLC governance experience. This analysis formed the foundation of the vision.

### 2. Competitive Intelligence
I performed a deep competitive analysis of ServiceNow's GRC and SecOps modules to understand their "governance reporting layer" approach. This allowed me to craft a strategic narrative positioning GitLab as the "governance enforcement layer," which resonated strongly with internal teams.

### 3. Interactive Prototyping
Static slides are not enough to communicate a complex vision. I built a high-fidelity, interactive prototype using Vue 3 and TypeScript to bring the concept to life. The prototype included 25 sample policies and an innovative "policy bundle" activation flow that made the vision tangible and exciting.

### 4. Internal Pitch & Socialization
With the analysis and prototype in hand, I created an 11-slide internal presentation and pitched the SDLC Governance Framework vision to PM and Engineering leadership. This kicked off the socialization process and began the work of building consensus and getting buy-in.

## The Solution
The output of this strategic effort was not a single feature, but a collection of artifacts that defined a new strategic direction for GitLab:

*   **A Strategic Vision Deck:** An 11-slide presentation that clearly articulated the problem, opportunity, and proposed solution.
*   **An Interactive Prototype:** A high-fidelity prototype that allowed stakeholders to see, touch, and feel the future of governance in GitLab.
*   **A Comprehensive Gap Analysis:** A detailed document outlining the 107 steps required to realize the full vision.
*   **A Design Partner Program:** An outreach program initiated to validate the vision with enterprise customers.

## Results & Impact
This strategic initiative is ongoing, but it has already had a significant impact:
*   **Shifted Company Strategy:** The SDLC Governance Framework is now a centerpiece strategic initiative for the Sec section.
*   **Secured Leadership Buy-in:** The vision was successfully pitched to and adopted by product and engineering leadership.
*   **Drove Customer Engagement:** The prototype and vision have been used in numerous high-level customer conversations, including with enterprise customers like NatWest ($10M deal).
